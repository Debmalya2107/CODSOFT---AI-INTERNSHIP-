Our Advanced Technology

AquaPure utilizes cutting-edge reverse osmosis technology combined with multiple filtration stages to deliver the highest quality water purification. Our systems incorporate:

  • Pre-filtration: Removes sediment, chlorine, and large particles
  • RO Membrane: Ultra-fine membrane removes dissolved salts and contaminants
  • UV Sterilization: Eliminates bacteria and viruses without chemicals
  • Activated Carbon: Improves taste and removes residual odors
  • Mineral Enhancement: Adds beneficial minerals back to purified water
  • Smart Monitoring: Real-time system performance tracking

Our research and development team continuously works on improving filtration efficiency, reducing water wastage, and incorporating smart features that make our systems user-friendly and environmentally conscious.

How Reverse Osmosis Works

1

Pre-Filtration

Water passes through sediment and carbon pre-filters to remove chlorine, sediment, and larger particles that could damage the RO membrane.

2

High Pressure

Water is pressurized and forced through the semi-permeable RO membrane, which blocks contaminants while allowing pure water molecules to pass.

3

Membrane Filtration

The RO membrane removes dissolved salts, heavy metals, chemicals, and other microscopic contaminants from the water.

4

Post-Filtration

Clean water passes through post-carbon filter and UV sterilization for final polishing and bacteria elimination.

5

Storage & Delivery

Purified water is stored in a sanitized tank and delivered through a dedicated faucet when needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our water purification systems

How often should I replace the filters?

Pre-filters should be replaced every 3-6 months, RO membrane every 1-2 years, and post-carbon filter every 6-12 months. The frequency depends on water quality and usage. Our systems have indicator lights to remind you when replacement is due.

What contaminants do AquaPure systems remove?

Our RO systems remove up to 99.9% of contaminants including bacteria, viruses, heavy metals (lead, mercury, arsenic), chemicals (chlorine, pesticides), dissolved salts, fluoride, and other harmful substances while retaining beneficial minerals.

How much water does the system waste?

Modern AquaPure systems have improved efficiency with a 1:2 pure to waste water ratio. For every gallon of pure water produced, approximately 2 gallons are used in the purification process. This is significantly better than older systems.

Do I need professional installation?

While some handy homeowners can install our systems, we recommend professional installation to ensure optimal performance and maintain your warranty. Our certified technicians ensure proper setup and provide complete system training.

What is the warranty coverage?

All AquaPure systems come with comprehensive warranties ranging from 1-10 years depending on the model. This covers manufacturing defects, pump failures, and electronic components. Consumable parts like filters are not covered under warranty.

Can the system work during power outages?

Most of our systems require electricity to operate the pump and UV sterilization. However, if you have a storage tank with purified water, you can access stored water during short power outages. Some models offer battery backup options.

How do I know when my system needs service?

Our smart systems have indicator lights for filter replacement and service alerts. Signs include reduced water flow, poor taste, unusual sounds, or error codes on the display panel. We also offer annual maintenance packages.

Is RO water safe for daily consumption?

Yes, RO water is completely safe for daily consumption. While it removes most minerals, a balanced diet provides necessary nutrients. Some of our models include mineral cartridges to add back beneficial minerals to the purified water.

What's the difference between RO and UV purification?

RO removes dissolved contaminants and reduces TDS levels, while UV kills bacteria and viruses. Our systems combine both technologies for comprehensive purification, ensuring removal of both physical contaminants and biological threats.

Can I install the system in any location?

Systems can be installed under sinks, in basements, or utility rooms. Requirements include access to water supply, drain, electricity, and adequate space for maintenance. Our technicians assess the best location during installation.
